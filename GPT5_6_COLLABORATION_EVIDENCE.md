# GPT-5.6 Collaboration Evidence

## Purpose

This document records the substantive use of GPT-5.6 during the OpenAI Build Week submission period for Rambam LawSys.

It supports the public repository and submission materials without disclosing private prompts, confidential knowledge files, copyrighted course materials, or full private conversation logs.

## Submission-period use

During July 13-21, 2026, GPT-5.6 was used as an active product-development and review partner across these workstreams:

### Legal-learning architecture

- Refined the source-before-synthesis workflow.
- Distinguished source content, lecturer interpretation, editorial synthesis, criticism, and uncertainty.
- Mapped the pathway from syllabi, cases, academic readings, lectures, notes, and exams to student-facing learning products.

### Provenance and attribution controls

- Developed controls against unsupported quotation and attribution.
- Refined missing-source and premature-completion vetoes.
- Clarified the separation between product readiness, method status, and deployment status.

### Quality assurance and failure-state analysis

- Designed adversarial scenarios for missing sources, uncertain attribution, incomplete units, premature assembly, and release-gate errors.
- Treated source coverage, analytical depth, product integration, RTL quality, and release readiness as separate quality dimensions.

### Educational product design

- Developed course study companions, source briefs, comparative doctrine maps, knowledge checks, exam practice, answer frameworks, and lecture companions.
- Preserved the rule that lecture-derived products do not automatically modify the canonical course notebook.

### Public demonstration and submission materials

- Helped specify and review the public source-coverage demonstration.
- Produced and reviewed Devpost submission copy, testing instructions, workflow-diagram content, repository documentation, the demo-video script, narration parity review, and the final video structure.

## Evidence retained by the project owner

The project owner retains timestamped ChatGPT project conversations and generated artifacts from the submission period, including product-development and QA conversations, public repository preparation records, submission drafting, diagram generation, and demo-video production records.

The full private conversation history is not published because it contains internal methodology, governance details, and non-public project material.

## Human decision responsibility

GPT-5.6 supported analysis, drafting, comparison, testing, and implementation review. The project owner retained responsibility for the methodology, product scope, source-fidelity requirements, quality thresholds, disclosure boundaries, and final decisions.
